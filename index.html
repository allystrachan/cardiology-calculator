Data Sources & Citations
All DRG reimbursement estimates are derived from the following publicly available sources
1 CMS IPPS Final Rule, FY 2025 & FY 2026 — MS-DRG relative weights, national standardized operating base rates, and wage index tables are published annually by the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services. Table 5 provides relative weights per MS-DRG; Tables 1A-1E provide the national standardized amounts.
CMS FY 2026 IPPS Final Rule Home Page
2 CMS Wage Index Tables (Table 3 by CBSA) — DHMC is located in Lebanon, NH (Grafton County), which falls in the Lebanon, NH-VT Micropolitan Statistical Area. The wage index value for this CBSA is used to adjust the labor-related share of the national base rate. FY 2025/2026 wage index for this area is approximately 0.9378.
CMS FY 2026 Wage Index Home Page
3 MS-DRG Definitions Manual (v42, FY 2025) — Defines how principal and secondary diagnoses, procedures, and complications/comorbidities (CC/MCC) determine DRG assignment for cardiac procedures. DRGs 001-002, 216-227, 231-236, 246-251, 258-260, 266-267 cover the cardiac procedures in this tool.
CMS MS-DRG Classifications and Software
4 IPPS Payment Formula — The full Medicare IPPS operating payment formula is: Payment = [Base Rate × (Labor Share × Wage Index + Non-Labor Share)] × DRG Weight. The labor-related share for FY 2026 is approximately 67.6%. The FY 2026 national operating standardized amount is approximately $6,411.
CRS Report: Medicare Hospital Payments — Adjusting for Variation in Geographic Area Wages
5 Medicaid Reimbursement Estimates — NH Medicaid typically reimburses at approximately 65–75% of Medicare rates for inpatient hospital services. This tool uses procedure-specific multipliers within that range. Exact Medicaid rates are set by the NH Department of Health and Human Services and vary by service.
NH DHHS Medicaid Program
6 Commercial/Private Insurance Estimates — Private payer reimbursements are estimated at 150–220% of Medicare rates, consistent with published research. The RAND Hospital Price Transparency Study (2024) and prior AHA analyses show that commercially negotiated rates for cardiac procedures average 180-200% of Medicare in the Northeast.
RAND Hospital Price Transparency Study
7 Typical Procedure Cost Ranges — Cost benchmarks are informed by published literature on cardiac surgery costs, including data from the Society of Thoracic Surgeons (STS) national database and HCUP (Healthcare Cost and Utilization Project) NIS data, adjusted for the New England region.
AHRQ HCUP Data | STS National Database
